Architectural Design

From minimalistic, practical household organization tools to elaborate, laser cut board game designs, one of my favorite groups I’ve had the privilege of working with is MiniWorld 3D. In an aim to make premium, educational models more accessible through 3D Printing, MiniWorld provides free, easy to print scale models. Here are some of my favorites that I’ve designed. These models were all created from scratch completely by me, no scans or repurposed models, using photographs and public blueprints.

Empire State Building

The Empire State Building was designed by Shreve, Lamb & Harmon and completed in 1931. The building has a roof height of 1,250 feet (380 m) and stands a total of 1,454 feet (443.2 m) tall, including its antenna. It stood as the world's tallest building for nearly 40 years until the completion of the World Trade Center's North Tower in 1970. Spin the models around, and click the AR button in the top left to see it in real life.

Mausoleum of Mohammed V

The Mausoleum of Mohammed V (Arabic: ضريح محمد الخامس‎) is a mausoleum located across from the Hassan Tower in Rabat, capital of Morocco. It contains the tombs of the Moroccan king Mohammed V and his two sons, late King Hassan II and Prince Abdallah. Spin the models around, and click the AR button in the top left to see it in real life.

Petronas Towers

The Petronas Towers, also known as the Petronas Twin Towers (Malay: Menara Petronas, or Menara Berkembar Petronas), are twin skyscrapers. They were the tallest buildings in the world from 1998 to 2004 and remain the tallest twin towers in the world. The buildings are a landmark of Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia. Spin the models around, and click the AR button in the top left to see it in real life.
