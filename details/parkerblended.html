Parker Blended

Description:

This pandemic has been hard for everyone, so I tried to do my part by staying home, watching Netflix...and learning iOS development and becoming a licensed Apple Developer. With the pandemic came the logistical challenges of back-to-school scheduling, so I built the Parker Blended app for Francis Parker High School to help students and faculty manage their daily COVID time schedules.

The app opens up with a series of walkthrough screens that let students pick from dropdown menus of all potential course options for every block of the day. After putting in their teacher’s names as well, a confirmation screen lets students make sure the schedule is locked in correctly (although it can be edited from the ‘profile’ tab later). From then on out, whenever the app is opened a calendar style UI will show their course-load for the day, and a tap on any of the courses will bring up a popup with the course title, teacher, start and end time, department, and class weight.

Technical Bits:

This app was coded in Swift in the Xcode IDE for iOS 13. Instead of using one of the myriad of app UI design platforms to design the app layout, I decided to force myself to learn how to navigate the Xcode storyboard design platform and SwiftUI so all the elements were either designed in the IDE or programmatically generated.

Challenges:

The push notifications were a nightmare. Creating an alternating A day B day schedule that didn’t overflow into the weekends was difficult enough, but to try and programmatically and procedurally generate push notifications at different times in the day for each user took forever to figure out. Similarly, there were lots of bugs with notifications being sent out twice at once, or reminding people to “go to class” for a free period.
